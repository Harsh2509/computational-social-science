Research

Political polarization on online platforms

The analysis of social media platforms to identify and report on ideological/political polarization is an important task that can help us better understand the nature of political discourse in online spaces. Political polarization is the divergence of political opinions, beliefs, attitudes, and stances between individuals and groups who hold opposing political views. In this problem, we will analyze social media/platforms to identify and report the ideological/political polarization, which is the divergence of political opinions, beliefs, attitudes, and stances of political adversaries.




Detecting Biases in social media

Social media bias is a topic of growing concern as social media platforms play an increasingly important role in shaping public opinion and political discourse. Bias on social media platforms can manifest in a variety of ways, including algorithmic bias, user bias, and content bias. These biases can result in differential treatment of users based on their demographic characteristics, such as gender, income level, racial affinity, national identity, and age. In this problem, we will explore, analyze and report the social media bias along various dimensions such as: gender, income level, racial affinity, national identity, age etc. This will help our society to become more neutral in terms of social media uses.




Impact of social media on health

The impact of social media on mental health, particularly depression, has become a topic of growing concern in recent years. While social media platforms can provide many benefits, such as connecting people and facilitating social interactions, they can also have negative impacts on mental health, including increased feelings of isolation, anxiety, and depression. In this problem, we will analyze the impact of social media on mental health (Depression). We can also explore the possibility of using social media in health care.




Misinformation/Fake news in social media spaces

Detecting and addressing misinformation and fake news on social media has become a crucial issue in today's digital landscape. Misinformation can have serious consequences, including spreading false information and causing harm to individuals and society as a whole. Therefore, it is important to identify and address misinformation on social media platforms. In this problem, we aim to detect the misinformation/fake news in social media. We will also explore the methods to reduce the impact of misinformation/fake information on the users of social media.




Communication dynamics during the disaster

The use of social media during disasters has become increasingly important as a means of communication and coordination among affected individuals and relief organizations. Social media platforms can provide real-time updates on the situation on the ground, connect individuals with emergency services and resources, and facilitate communication and coordination among affected communities. In this problem, we will explore the uses of social media/networks for communication dynamics during the disaster. We can develop an architecture to use social media for helping people during disasters.




Influence maximization in social networks

Influence maximization is a problem in social network analysis that involves identifying a small set of influential nodes, known as seed nodes, that can maximize the spread of information or behavior in a social network. The goal of influence maximization is to identify the most influential nodes in a network in order to maximize the impact of a message or behavior. Here, we can propose various techniques/ algorithms for Influence maximization which can be defined as the problem of finding a small subset of nodes (seed nodes) in a social network that could maximize the spread of influence.




Link prediction in social networks

Link prediction is a problem in social network analysis that involves predicting the likelihood of the existence of a link between two nodes in a network. The goal of link prediction is to identify missing or future links between nodes in a social network, which can be useful in a variety of applications, such as recommendation systems, social network analysis, and marketing. Link prediction aims to estimate the probability of existence (or formation) of each of the non-existing links in the network in order to identify a set of missing or future links between the users. Here, we can propose techniques/applications for/of link prediction in social networks.




Community detection in social networks

Community detection is a fundamental problem in social network analysis consisting, roughly speaking, in unsupervised dividing social actors (modeled as nodes in a social graph) with certain social connections (modeled as edges in the social graph) into densely knitted and highly related groups with each group well separated from the others. Here, we can propose techniques/applications for/of community detection in social networks.




Online harm detection

Detecting and analyzing harmful, toxic, and abusive content on social media is a crucial task in ensuring a safe and positive online environment. Harmful content can include hate speech, cyberbullying, violent or graphic content, and other forms of inappropriate or offensive material. In this problem, we will detect and analyze the harmful/toxic/abusive contents on social media.




Recommender systems

A recommendation system is an artificial intelligence or AI algorithm, usually associated with machine learning, that uses Big Data to suggest or recommend additional products to consumers. These can be based on various criteria, including past purchases, search history, demographic information, and other factors. Here, we can propose techniques/applications for/of recommender systems in social media.




Question Answering

Creating an automated answering system involves developing a computer program that can extract the most relevant information from available data sources to provide an accurate answer to a given question. The system typically uses natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning (ML) techniques to analyze large datasets and identify the most relevant information based on the input question. We will create an automated answering system by extracting the most relevant text from the available/online data for a given question.




Disease outbreak detection using Social media data

Creating an alarming system for viral diseases using social media data involves analyzing large amounts of social media data to identify potential outbreaks of viral diseases in real-time. This approach can complement traditional disease surveillance methods, which may have longer lags in detecting outbreaks. We can create the alarming system for viral diseases using social media data, and can establish the relationship between disease outbreak and other factors(like temperature, location etc)
